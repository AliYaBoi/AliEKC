Consoles: NES, Sega Master System, Atari 7800, and the Atari XEGS The third-generation consoles used 8-bit processors which began in 1983. This allowed the graphics to run between 3 to 5 bits which enabled to 8 to 32 colours at resolutions of 256 x 240 pixels, five audio channels, and more advanced graphics capabilities including sprites and tiles rather than block-based graphics of the second generation. 3rd generation console controllers had directional pads or D-pads for short. These gen 3 consoles ended the video game crash of 1983.
