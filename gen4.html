Generation 4, being between 1987 and 2004, includes consoles such as the TurboGrafx-16, the Neo Genesis, and the Super NES. Some hardware changes include:

 The companies creating the consoles back then (Hudson Soft, Sega, SNK Corp, and Nintendo), created their games on CD-ROMS to offer more space than game cartridges and could allow full motion video and other detailed audio works to be used in games. Console manufacturers would then create add-ons to their consoles that could read the CD-ROMs and play them, including NEC’s TurboGrafx-CD add-on. Costs were high, nearing the same price as the console itself.

The 4th generation also overlapped into the 5th generation with Sony's Playstation.
