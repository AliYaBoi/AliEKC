Across 9 console generations, presenting numerous technological innovations and spanning over 50 years, PC retains its relevancy as the preferred gaming device of millions around the globe. The complete customization of hardware a PC provides has allowed it to remain at the forefront of the gaming industry, giving it the longest term support of any gaming platform. Below, you will find a timeline of events that has helped to shape not only PC gaming, but the industry as a whole.

 

1960s

1962 - MIT students create Spacewar

1970s

1972 - Allan Alcorn creates Pong, releasing under Atari as an arcade machine.
circa. 1979 - First text adventures are created, allowing for keyboard interactivity.

1980s

1981 - The “IBM Personal Computer” is released as the first widely-available consumer PC. Early released games included Microsoft Adventure and Microsoft Flight Simulator. The operating system used was PC-DOS.
1982 – The Commodore 64 is released to consumers, dominating the low-end computing market internationally for much of the 1980s. Its release also birthed the “demoscene”, a culture in which groups or individuals produce audiovisual presentations to showcase their skills. This scene would closely tie to the online piracy scene, with cracking groups creating their own demos, known as “cracktros” to advertise themselves.
1985 – While working under the Academy of Sciences of the Soviet Union, Alexey Pajitnov creates Tetris for the Elektronika 60. After many legal complications involving the soviet government, it is released internationally, eventually becoming one of the best-selling game franchises of all time.
1987 – Microsoft creates their own version of PC-DOS, called MS-DOS. This would eventually become the basis for Microsoft Windows.

1990s

1992 – Wolfenstein 3D released, credited for creating the FPS genre.
1993 – DOOM is released, popularizing the FPS genre.
1998 – Published by Sierra Studios, Valve releases Half-Life to critical acclaim. Epic Games creates Unreal Engine, eventually leading to the development of many successful games.
1999 – Counter-Strike is created as a mod for Half-Life.

2000s

2000 – Valve hires the Counter-Strike development team, releasing the game as a standalone product.
2003 – Valve releases Steam to offer patches and updates to their games.
2004 – Valve releases Half-Life 2, the first game to be digitally released on Steam. World of Warcraft becomes the most popular MMORPG, with over 12 million subscriptions by the end of the 2000s.

2010s

2011 – Markus “Notch” Persson releases Minecraft after 2 years of development, eventually becoming the best-selling game of all time.
2014 – Epic Games releases Unreal Engine 4 after 11 years of development, quickly becoming an industry standard for 3D game development.
2017 – Epic Games releases Fortnite. Capitalizing on the recent trend of the battle royale genre, they release Fortnite: Battle Royale as a side mode a few months later, to overwhelming success.

2020s

2022 – Valve releases the Steam Deck, a handheld device designed as a mainstream way to play PC games on the go. Alongside this, Valve’s experimental Proton technology becomes mainstream, vastly improving game compatibility on Linux machines.
