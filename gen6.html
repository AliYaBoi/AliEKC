Generation 6, between 2005 and 2017, consisted of the Sega Dreamcast, the PlayStation 2, the GameCube, and the original Xbox. Some hardware changes include:

Technology of these consoles started to catch up to the power of the PCs of their times. These consoles expanded their use of DVDs with greater capacity. 

Some examples of changes based on previous consoles include:

Dreamcast:

You were able to connect to a modem to play online with your friends. First released in 1998. It was the last system created by Sega as they could not keep up with Sony. 

PlayStation:

The PlayStation 2 was the first console to add support for DVD playback to CD-ROM, as well as maintaining backward compatibility with games from their previous PlayStation library. 

Nintendo:

Nintendo entered the GameCube into the sixth generation in 2001, its first system to use optical discs based on the miniDVD format. Adapters were available to allow the console to connect to the Internet via Broadband or Modem. 

Xbox:

Xbox entered the first console in 2001, claiming the PlayStation 2 a huge threat, the Xbox was designed via Microsoft’s personal computer experience. They used an operating system based on its Windows and DirectX features, utilizing a hard disk for save game storage, built-in Ethernet functionality, and they created the online service, Xbox Live, to support multiplayer games.
