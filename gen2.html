The second-generation console started in 1976 and ended in 1984. This is where the Microprocessors was developed, this is also where the graphics increased to 3 bit/8 colour and the resolution was around 160 x 192. Game was burned on read only memory (ROM)

 

Fairchild semiconductor

The first generation 2 console made was called the Fairchild Semiconductor’s Fairchild VES (later renamed Fairchild Channel F) this was the first CPU-based console

In the same year Atari had released their own CPU console which was known as Atari 2600 which dominated the second generation market due to its large amount of games it sold. 

other gen 2 console was made like the Magnavox’s odyssey 2(1978) and Intellivison (1980), Intellivision utilized a unique processor that run wider instruction allowing more variety and speed. 

ColecoVision

 

Another console released was the ColecoVision in 1982 it launched with twelve different game one of them was a licensed Nintendo game called Donkey Kong however it was hit by the video game crash of 1983 and the console was discontinued.
